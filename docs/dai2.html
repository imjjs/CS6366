Basic Model

Yi Li

Tags: distributed AI

Definition

  • \(\Gamma = \{\gamma_1, \gamma_2, ..., \gamma_n\}\) is a collection of dynamic agents and \(\gamma_i\) is the i-th agent.
  • \(G = (\Gamma, E)\) is a time-invariant undirected connected graph describing neighborhood relations on \(\Gamma\), where \(E \subset \Gamma \times \Gamma\) is the edge-set. By undirected we mean that \(\forall (\gamma_i, \gamma_j) \in E, (\gamma_j, \gamma_i) \in E\). The neighborhood of \(\gamma_i\) is denoted by \(N(\gamma_i) = \{\gamma_j\in \Gamma : (\gamma_i,\gamma_j) \in E\}\).
  • \(S\) is the state space and \(s_t(\gamma_i) \in S\) denotes the state of agent \(\gamma_i\) at time \(t\), while \(\vec{s_t} = (s_t(\gamma_1), s_t(\gamma_2),..., s_t(\gamma_n)) \in S^n\) is the state vector for all agents at time \(t\).
  • A distributed and stationary control policy of i-th agent at time \(t\) is denoted by \(u^{(i)}_t: S^n \rightarrow A\), where $A$ is the action set. By distributed we mean the agent makes decisions according the states of its neighbors ( \(u^{(i)}_t(\vec{s_t}) = u^{(i)}_t(\vec{s'_t})\) if \(\forall j, \gamma_j \in N(\gamma_i)\) and \(s_t(\gamma_j) = s'_t(\gamma_j)\))
